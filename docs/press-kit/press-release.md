## Format B — Standard release (1-page, for press distribution)

> **FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**
>
> # Webarmonium — A Musical Instrument That Plays the Web
> ## A new persistent net-art work by Paolo Quaranta
>
> Varese, Italy — [TODO — release date], 2026 — Italian artist **Paolo Quaranta** announces *Webarmonium*, a browser-based networked musical instrument that translates real-time public web activity into sound, and lets remote strangers compose together over a shared browser session. The work is live and continuous at **webarmonium.net**.
>
> *Webarmonium* listens to three open public data streams—Wikipedia's Recent Changes feed, Hacker News's new stories, and GitHub's Public Events—and translates each event it hears into a note through a fixed, deterministic mapping. The composition that emerges is the unconscious performance of millions of strangers who will never hear it. A live ticker on the page surfaces the specific web event that triggered each note ("Napoleon edited · C#4"), making the data → sound causality immediately legible.
>
> A second mode opens collaborative rooms where up to four remote participants meet to play the instrument intentionally—through gestures (tap, hold, drag), through programmable step sequencers, through three fully synthesized drum kits, through a sound-design panel for shaping each voice. An autonomous audition mode generates phrases on golden-ratio timing, so the instrument continues to perform when no human is touching it.
>
> The mapping is deterministic throughout. There is no randomness, only the structured translation of human activity—conscious and unconscious, programmatic and gestural—into sound.
>
> *Webarmonium* asks what becomes of musical authorship when the score is written, in real time, by everyone, no one, and the instrument itself.
>
> The work was developed entirely through iterative dialogue with a large language model and is built in vanilla JavaScript with the Web Audio API, Tone.js, Socket.io, and PixiJS. It exists only at webarmonium.net—not packaged, not downloadable, not installable. There is no admission, no account, no purchase.
>
> ---
>
> **About the artist**
> Paolo Quaranta (b. 1968, Viareggio; lives and works in Varese, Italy) is a self-taught Italian artist working between generative systems, networked instruments, and sound. A classically trained flutist, prog-rock vocalist, and builder of analog modular synthesizers, he turned to digital generative work in 2022 under the pseudonym "OldFart," releasing more than forty pieces of digital freehand and generative art across Tezos and Ethereum. *Webarmonium* (2026) is his first artwork released under his own name and his first persistent, browser-based net-art instrument. He works as an educator with people with disabilities.
