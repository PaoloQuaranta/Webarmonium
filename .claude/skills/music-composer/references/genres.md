# Music Genres Reference

## Classical

### Baroque (1600-1750)
- Composers: Bach, Vivaldi, Handel
- Characteristics: Ornate, contrapuntal, figured bass
- Forms: Fugue, concerto grosso, suite
- Harmony: Functional tonality, sequences
- Tempo: Often moderate to fast, dance-based

### Classical (1750-1820)
- Composers: Mozart, Haydn, early Beethoven
- Characteristics: Clear structure, balanced phrases, melodic
- Forms: Sonata, symphony, string quartet
- Harmony: Clear tonic-dominant relationships
- Texture: Homophonic, lighter than Baroque

### Romantic (1820-1900)
- Composers: Chopin, Liszt, Brahms, Wagner
- Characteristics: Emotional expression, chromaticism, rubato
- Forms: Expanded classical forms, tone poem
- Harmony: Extended tonality, rich chromaticism
- Dynamics: Wide range, dramatic contrasts

### Impressionist (1890-1930)
- Composers: Debussy, Ravel
- Characteristics: Atmospheric, coloristic, ambiguous tonality
- Scales: Whole tone, pentatonic, modes
- Harmony: Extended chords (9ths, 11ths, 13ths), parallel motion
- Texture: Blurred, shimmering effects

### 20th Century/Contemporary
- Composers: Stravinsky, Schoenberg, Glass, Reich
- Characteristics: Experimental, diverse styles
- Techniques: Serialism, minimalism, aleatoric, extended techniques
- Harmony: Atonal, polytonal, or neo-tonal

## Jazz

### Swing/Big Band (1930s-1940s)
- Tempo: Medium-fast, swung eighth notes
- Instrumentation: Large ensemble (saxes, trumpets, trombones, rhythm)
- Characteristics: Call-and-response, riffs, arranged
- Harmony: ii-V-I progressions, dominant 7th chords

### Bebop (1940s-1950s)
- Tempo: Fast, complex
- Characteristics: Virtuosic solos, angular melodies
- Harmony: Complex, altered chords, rapid changes
- Key players: Charlie Parker, Dizzy Gillespie

### Cool Jazz (1950s)
- Tempo: Relaxed, moderate
- Characteristics: Smoother, more relaxed than bebop
- Tone: Softer, less aggressive
- Key players: Miles Davis, Chet Baker

### Modal Jazz (1960s)
- Characteristics: Based on modes rather than chord changes
- Harmony: Static or slow-moving, emphasis on scales
- Example: "So What" by Miles Davis
- Scales: Dorian, Phrygian, Mixolydian

### Fusion (1970s-present)
- Characteristics: Jazz + rock/funk/R&B elements
- Instrumentation: Electric instruments, synthesizers
- Rhythm: Rock/funk grooves, odd meters
- Key players: Weather Report, Herbie Hancock

### Contemporary/Smooth Jazz
- Characteristics: Accessible, melodic, polished production
- Harmony: Extended chords, often static
- Tempo: Moderate, groovy
- Commercial appeal

## Rock

### Rock & Roll (1950s)
- Tempo: Upbeat, driving
- Structure: 12-bar blues, verse-chorus
- Instruments: Guitar, bass, drums, sometimes piano
- Characteristics: Energetic, rebellious, danceable

### Blues Rock (1960s-present)
- Scales: Pentatonic, blues scale
- Harmony: I-IV-V, blues progressions
- Characteristics: Guitar-focused, expressive bends
- Key artists: Cream, Led Zeppelin, SRV

### Progressive Rock (1970s)
- Characteristics: Complex structures, odd meters, instrumental virtuosity
- Influences: Classical, jazz
- Length: Extended compositions (10+ minutes)
- Key bands: Yes, Genesis, King Crimson

### Punk (1970s-1980s)
- Tempo: Fast, aggressive
- Characteristics: Simple, raw, DIY aesthetic
- Chords: Power chords (root + 5th)
- Structure: Short songs (2-3 minutes)

### Metal (1970s-present)
- Characteristics: Heavy distortion, power chords, aggressive
- Tempo: Varies (slow doom to fast thrash)
- Tuning: Often drop or low tunings
- Subgenres: Thrash, death, black, power, doom

### Alternative/Indie (1980s-present)
- Characteristics: Non-mainstream, diverse, experimental
- Production: Often lo-fi or unique
- Structure: Varies widely

## Electronic

### Techno (1980s-present)
- BPM: 120-135
- Characteristics: Repetitive 4/4 beat, synthesized sounds
- Structure: Minimal, gradually evolving
- Elements: 909 drums, TB-303 bass, evolving pads

### House (1980s-present)
- BPM: 120-130
- Characteristics: 4-on-the-floor kick, soulful samples
- Structure: Intro-build-drop-break-drop
- Subgenres: Deep house, tech house, progressive house

### Trance (1990s-present)
- BPM: 125-150
- Characteristics: Build-ups, breakdowns, euphoric melodies
- Structure: Long builds with dramatic releases
- Elements: Arpeggiated synths, sweeping pads

### Drum & Bass (1990s-present)
- BPM: 160-180
- Characteristics: Fast breakbeats, heavy bass
- Rhythm: Syncopated, complex drum patterns
- Bass: Sub-bass, reese bass

### Dubstep (2000s-present)
- BPM: 140 (half-time feel = 70)
- Characteristics: Wobble bass, sparse drums
- Structure: Build-up to bass drop
- Elements: LFO modulated bass, syncopated rhythms

### Ambient (1970s-present)
- BPM: Slow or none
- Characteristics: Atmospheric, textural, minimal beats
- Purpose: Background, meditative, cinematic
- Elements: Pads, drones, field recordings

## Pop & R&B

### Pop (1950s-present)
- Structure: Verse-chorus, often with bridge
- Tempo: Varies (typically 100-130 BPM)
- Characteristics: Catchy melodies, accessible, radio-friendly
- Harmony: Simple (I-V-vi-IV common)

### R&B/Soul (1960s-present)
- Characteristics: Smooth vocals, groove-oriented
- Harmony: Rich chords (7ths, 9ths, 11ths)
- Rhythm: Syncopated, laid-back groove
- Influences: Gospel, blues, jazz

### Funk (1970s-present)
- Characteristics: Groove-based, syncopated, rhythmic
- Focus: Rhythm section (bass + drums)
- Harmony: Often based on single chord or vamp
- Elements: Wah guitar, slap bass, horn stabs

## Hip-Hop

### Old School (1980s)
- BPM: 90-110
- Characteristics: Breakbeats, samples, simple drums
- Production: Drum machines, samplers
- Structure: Loop-based

### Boom Bap (1990s)
- Characteristics: Hard-hitting drums, jazz samples
- Kick: Punchy, prominent
- Snare: Crisp, loud
- Feel: Head-nodding groove

### Trap (2000s-present)
- BPM: 130-170 (half-time feel)
- Characteristics: 808 bass, hi-hat rolls, snare rolls
- Production: Heavy low-end, sparse arrangement
- Rhythm: Triplet hi-hats

## Latin

### Salsa
- Time: 4/4, clave-based
- Instruments: Congas, timbales, piano, brass
- Clave: 3-2 or 2-3 pattern (rhythmic foundation)
- Tempo: Medium-fast, danceable

### Bossa Nova
- Time: 4/4, syncopated
- Characteristics: Smooth, jazzy, sophisticated
- Guitar: Nylon string, specific rhythmic pattern
- Harmony: Jazz-influenced (7ths, 9ths)

### Reggaeton
- BPM: 90-100
- Characteristics: Dem Bow rhythm, synthesized production
- Pattern: Boom-ch-boom-chick
- Bass: Simple, repetitive

## World Music

### African
- Characteristics: Polyrhythm, call-and-response, percussion-heavy
- Scales: Pentatonic, varied regional scales
- Instruments: Djembe, talking drum, kora, mbira

### Middle Eastern
- Scales: Maqamat (modal systems with microtones)
- Characteristics: Ornamentation, improvisation
- Instruments: Oud, qanun, ney, darbuka

### Indian Classical
- Characteristics: Raga (melodic framework), tala (rhythmic cycle)
- Improvisation: Extensive, structured
- Instruments: Sitar, tabla, tanpura

## Film/Game Scoring

### Orchestral Score
- Characteristics: Leitmotifs (character themes), emotional cues
- Instrumentation: Full orchestra
- Purpose: Underscoring, emotional enhancement

### Ambient/Atmospheric
- Characteristics: Subtle, non-intrusive, loopable
- Purpose: Background, mood setting
- Elements: Pads, minimal melody

### Action/Epic
- Characteristics: Driving rhythms, large orchestration, brass
- Dynamics: Loud, intense, climactic
- Purpose: Excitement, tension, heroism
