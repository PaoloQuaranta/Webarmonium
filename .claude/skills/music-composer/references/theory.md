# Music Theory Reference

## Intervals

### Interval Quality and Distance
- Perfect Unison (P1): 0 semitones
- Minor 2nd (m2): 1 semitone
- Major 2nd (M2): 2 semitones  
- Minor 3rd (m3): 3 semitones
- Major 3rd (M3): 4 semitones
- Perfect 4th (P4): 5 semitones
- Tritone (TT/Aug4/dim5): 6 semitones
- Perfect 5th (P5): 7 semitones
- Minor 6th (m6): 8 semitones
- Major 6th (M6): 9 semitones
- Minor 7th (m7): 10 semitones
- Major 7th (M7): 11 semitones
- Perfect Octave (P8): 12 semitones

## Scales

### Major Scale
Formula: W-W-H-W-W-W-H (Whole-Whole-Half-Whole-Whole-Whole-Half)
Intervals from root: 0-2-4-5-7-9-11

### Natural Minor Scale  
Formula: W-H-W-W-H-W-W
Intervals: 0-2-3-5-7-8-10

### Harmonic Minor
Formula: W-H-W-W-H-W+H-H
Intervals: 0-2-3-5-7-8-11
Characteristic: Raised 7th degree, creates exotic sound

### Melodic Minor
Ascending: W-H-W-W-W-W-H (0-2-3-5-7-9-11)
Descending: Same as natural minor
Used in jazz extensively

### Modes of Major Scale

1. **Ionian** (Major): W-W-H-W-W-W-H
   - Character: Bright, happy, stable

2. **Dorian**: W-H-W-W-W-H-W
   - Character: Minor with raised 6th, jazzy, modal
   - Example: D Dorian = D E F G A B C

3. **Phrygian**: H-W-W-W-H-W-W
   - Character: Dark, Spanish/flamenco flavor
   - Example: E Phrygian = E F G A B C D

4. **Lydian**: W-W-W-H-W-W-H
   - Character: Bright, dreamy, raised 4th
   - Example: F Lydian = F G A B C D E

5. **Mixolydian**: W-W-H-W-W-H-W
   - Character: Major with flat 7th, bluesy
   - Example: G Mixolydian = G A B C D E F

6. **Aeolian** (Natural Minor): W-H-W-W-H-W-W
   - Character: Sad, serious, dark

7. **Locrian**: H-W-W-H-W-W-W
   - Character: Unstable, diminished feel
   - Rarely used as tonal center

### Pentatonic Scales

**Major Pentatonic**: W-W-m3-W-m3 (0-2-4-7-9)
- Use: Pop, rock, country, blues
- Very consonant, no avoid notes

**Minor Pentatonic**: m3-W-W-m3-W (0-3-5-7-10)
- Use: Rock, blues, metal solos
- Foundation of blues scale

### Blues Scale
0-3-5-6-7-10 (Minor pentatonic + blue note)
Blue note (b5) adds characteristic blues flavor

### Exotic Scales

**Whole Tone**: W-W-W-W-W-W (0-2-4-6-8-10)
- Dreamy, impressionistic (Debussy)
- No perfect 5th

**Diminished** (Octatonic): H-W-H-W-H-W-H-W
- Symmetric, jazz, modern classical

**Hungarian Minor**: W-H-W+H-H-H-W+H-H
- Exotic, dramatic

**Japanese Pentatonic**: H-M3-W-H-M3
- Traditional Japanese music

## Chords

### Triads

**Major**: Root + M3 + P5 (0-4-7)
- Sound: Happy, stable, complete

**Minor**: Root + m3 + P5 (0-3-7)
- Sound: Sad, somber, introspective

**Diminished**: Root + m3 + dim5 (0-3-6)
- Sound: Tense, unstable, needs resolution

**Augmented**: Root + M3 + Aug5 (0-4-8)
- Sound: Tense, mysterious, floating

### Seventh Chords

**Major 7th** (Maj7): 0-4-7-11
- Sound: Jazzy, dreamy, sophisticated

**Dominant 7th** (7): 0-4-7-10
- Sound: Bluesy, wants to resolve, tension

**Minor 7th** (m7): 0-3-7-10
- Sound: Mellow, jazzy, smooth

**Minor 7th♭5** (m7♭5): 0-3-6-10
- Sound: Dark, jazzy, half-diminished

**Diminished 7th** (dim7): 0-3-6-9
- Sound: Very tense, symmetric

### Extended Chords

**9th Chords**: Add 9th (M2) above root
- Major 9th: 0-4-7-11-14
- Dominant 9th: 0-4-7-10-14

**11th Chords**: Add 11th (P4) above root
**13th Chords**: Add 13th (M6) above root

### Suspended Chords

**sus2**: Root + M2 + P5 (0-2-7)
- Open, unresolved, modern

**sus4**: Root + P4 + P5 (0-5-7)
- Tension, wants to resolve to major

## Harmonic Functions

### Tonal Functions
- **Tonic (I)**: Home, stable, resolution
- **Subdominant (IV)**: Preparation, moves away from tonic
- **Dominant (V)**: Tension, strong pull to tonic

### Cadences

**Authentic Cadence (V-I)**: Strong, final
**Plagal Cadence (IV-I)**: "Amen" cadence, gentle
**Deceptive Cadence (V-vi)**: Surprise, avoids expected resolution
**Half Cadence (X-V)**: Pause, opens-ended

## Voice Leading

### Principles
1. Move voices by smallest intervals possible
2. Avoid parallel 5ths and octaves
3. Keep common tones between chords
4. Resolve tendency tones (7→8, 4→3)

### Inversions
- Root position: Root in bass
- First inversion: 3rd in bass (notation: 6)
- Second inversion: 5th in bass (notation: 6/4)
- Third inversion (7th chords): 7th in bass (notation: 4/2)

## Rhythm & Meter

### Time Signatures
- 4/4: Common time, 4 beats per bar
- 3/4: Waltz time, 3 beats per bar
- 6/8: Compound meter, 2 groups of 3
- 5/4: Asymmetrical, prog rock
- 7/8: Complex, odd meter

### Note Values (in 4/4)
- Whole: 4 beats
- Half: 2 beats
- Quarter: 1 beat
- Eighth: 0.5 beats
- Sixteenth: 0.25 beats

### Syncopation
Emphasis on weak beats or off-beats
Creates rhythmic interest and groove

## Form & Structure

### Common Forms

**Binary (AB)**: Two contrasting sections
**Ternary (ABA)**: Return to opening material
**Rondo (ABACA)**: Recurring main theme
**Sonata**: Exposition-Development-Recapitulation
**Theme & Variations**: Theme followed by variations
**Through-composed**: Continuously new material

### Pop/Rock Song Structures
- Verse-Chorus: V-C-V-C-B-C
- AABA: 32-bar form (jazz standard)
- Verse-PreChorus-Chorus: V-PC-C-V-PC-C-B-C

## Key Relationships

### Circle of Fifths
Clockwise (sharps): C-G-D-A-E-B-F#-C#
Counterclockwise (flats): C-F-Bb-Eb-Ab-Db-Gb-Cb

### Relative Keys
- Relative minor: 3 semitones below major
- Share same key signature
- Example: C major ↔ A minor

### Parallel Keys
- Same tonic, different mode
- Example: C major ↔ C minor

## Modulation

### Common Techniques
- **Pivot chord**: Shared chord between keys
- **Direct/Abrupt**: Sudden key change
- **Sequential**: Repeat pattern in new key
- **Common tone**: Hold one note, change harmony

### Related Keys (easy to modulate to)
- Dominant (V)
- Subdominant (IV)
- Relative major/minor
- Parallel major/minor
